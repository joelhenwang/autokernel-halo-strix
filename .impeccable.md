## Design Context

### Users
ML engineers monitoring multi-day GPU training runs on desktop monitors. Checking training health, spotting instability, comparing checkpoints. Used on a local network browser on the training machine itself — no mobile, no public-facing.

### Brand Personality
Clean, technical, confident. Like a well-designed scientific instrument — every element serves a purpose, nothing decorative. The interface should feel like it was built by someone who understands the data, not by a SaaS company trying to sell you something.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone:** Quiet precision. A research observatory, not a corporate dashboard.
- **Theme:** Dark — used in focused work sessions, often late, monitoring long runs.
- **Density:** Balanced — enough data to be useful at a glance, enough breathing room to not overwhelm. Not Grafana-dense, not wandb-spacious.
- **Anti-references:** Grafana (too busy, plugin overload), TensorBoard (clunky, dated, poor typography), Weights & Biases (corporate SaaS feel, too many features competing for attention). None of these.
- **References:** Scientific paper figures. Bloomberg terminal's information clarity (not its density). Dieter Rams — "as little design as possible." The calm of a well-set typographic page.

### Design Principles
1. **Data first** — Every pixel serves the data. No decorative elements, no branding, no marketing.
2. **Quiet confidence** — The interface should feel authoritative without shouting. Subtle hierarchy, not bold contrasts.
3. **Typographic clarity** — Numbers and labels are the primary content. They must be instantly readable.
4. **Purposeful motion** — Animate only to convey state changes (data refresh, zoom transitions). No gratuitous effects.
5. **Honest presentation** — Show the real data, including uncertainty and noise. No smoothing-by-default that hides problems.
