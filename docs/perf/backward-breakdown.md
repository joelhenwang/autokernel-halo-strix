### rocBLAS GEMM share inside backward

The `Cijk_*` kernels are rocBLAS-generated GEMMs used by both forward and backward:

| Kernel | CUDA time | % step | Shape class |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Cijk_Ailk_Bljk_HHS_BH_Bias (MT96x96) | 5.487 s | 13.63% | mixed fwd+bwd |
| Cijk_Alik_Bljk_HHS_BH_Bias (MT128x8) | 3.448 s | 8.57% | likely transposed-B (bwd weight grad) |
| Cijk_Ailk_Bjlk_HHS_BH_Bias (MT64x96) | 2.988 s | 7.42% | likely transposed-A (bwd input grad) |
| Cijk_Ailk_Bljk_HHS_BH_Bias (MT64x96) | 1.096 s | 2.72% | fwd shape |
| Cijk_Alik_Bljk_HHS_BH_Bias (MT96x12) | 0.605 s | 1.50% | bwd-tagged |

`HHS_BH_` suffix = half-half-single (fp16 inputs, fp32 accumulator). `Ailk_Bljk` = contiguous A, contiguous B. `Alik_Bljk` / `Ailk_Bjlk` = one operand transposed — **these are the backward-specific shapes** (`g @ w.T` and `x.T @ g` for weight gradient).

**Backward-tagged rocBLAS share:** 8.57 + 7.42 + 1.50 = **17.49% of step wall** spent in transposed-shape matmuls for gradient computation.

**Forward-tagged rocBLAS share:** 13.63 + 2.72 = **16.35%** in non-transposed shapes.

**Sum:** ~34% of step wall is rocBLAS matmul across fwd+bwd. Matches the aten::mm 37.85% total (residual ~4% is small GEMMs + NorMuon NS matmuls).

## Implications for T-2.1 (compiled autograd)

### What compiled autograd could attack

`torch._dynamo.config.compiled_autograd = True` compiles the backward graph as one unit. Candidates:
- **CompiledFunctionBackward wrapper overhead** (~35% minus inner kernels): likely 2-5% of step wall is dispatcher + autograd engine overhead that compiled-autograd can collapse into the backward graph.
- **autograd::engine::evaluate_function overhead**: 0.4% observed, small but pure overhead.
- **MmBackward0 non-compiled** (5.2%): these are outside current compile zones (likely NorMuon NS). Compiled autograd might pull them into the unified backward graph.

### What compiled autograd cannot attack

- rocBLAS GEMMs: non-addressable (already optimal)
- LogsumexpBackward: already a single kernel, compiled autograd wouldn't change it
- CausalConv1dFnBackward: external C++ extension, opaque to Dynamo
- Triton kernels generated by Inductor: already integrated

### Realistic compiled-autograd upside

| Lower bound | Realistic | Upper bound |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 2% | 5-8% | 12-15% |

The upper bound assumes compiled autograd also enables some cross-kernel fusion in the backward graph that's currently segmented by `register_autograd` boundaries. Lower bound assumes only the wrapper overhead is recoverable.

**Risk:** ROCm 7.12 + `register_autograd` custom_ops + DDP bucket hooks is an untested combination. Feasibility unknown.

## Forward vs backward symmetry analysis

Rough FLOP count per optimizer step (batch=16, block=512, accum=8, DDP=2):

| Op category | Forward FLOPs | Backward FLOPs | Ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| FFN matmuls (w_gate_up + w_down) | 38.65 GF/layer | 2× = 77.3 GF/layer | 2:1 |
| Attention matmuls | 12.88 GF/layer | 2× = 25.76 GF/layer | 2:1 |
| Total per layer | 51.53 GF | 103.06 GF | 2:1 |

Observed wall-time ratio (backward/forward):
- CompiledFunction fwd: 6.523 s
- CompiledFunctionBackward: 14.213 s
- Ratio: **2.18×**

Very close to the theoretical 2:1 FLOP ratio. This is a **good** signal — it means backward is running at ~roughly forward's efficiency per-FLOP, with only ~10% overhead beyond the inevitable 2× work.

**Implication:** there's no obvious "backward is 5× inefficient" waste to recover. The 35% backward bucket is mostly intrinsic work, not overhead. Compiled autograd upside is probably toward the 2-5% end of the range above, not 12-15%.

## Revised compiled-autograd priority

Given this analysis, compiled autograd is still worth T-2.1's 5-6 day budget because:
1. Even 2-5% compounds with T-1.1 (fused-zloss ~3-5%) + T-2.3 (DDP overlap ~3-5%) to approach the 36-38k target
2. It's the only lever that touches the backward bucket at all
3. Infrastructure value — once working, it's permanent and enables future backward-specific optimizations

But the **expected value is lower than our prior estimate** (+5-15% → +2-8%). This revises our Stack A outcome estimate:

| Prior estimate | Revised estimate |
|---:|---:|
| Stack A: 36-38k | Stack A: **33-36k** |
| Stack B: 38-41k | Stack B: **35-39k** |

**40k is less likely than we thought before this analysis.** The backward bucket is not an overhead problem; it's doing 2× the forward work at ~forward efficiency. Most of our remaining throughput budget is in loss + DDP + sync removal.

## Conclusion

**Backward is working correctly.** 35.3% of step wall doing 2× the forward's FLOPs at near-theoretical efficiency.

**Compiled autograd remains the best single lever** but with revised expectations: 2-8% realistic, not 5-15%.

**40k from 31k is at the boundary of the addressable budget.** Achievable only if every T-1/T-2/T-3 lever delivers its upper bound simultaneously — which is unlikely. 35-37k is the honest target; 40k is aspirational.

This data strengthens our §2.3 concern to the research engineer that the 40k framing needs recalibration.
