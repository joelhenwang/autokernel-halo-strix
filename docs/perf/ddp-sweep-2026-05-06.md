# DDP Config Sweep — Throughput Optimization (2026-05-06)

## Summary

Three isolated sweeps run in parallel across Machine A (12 configs) and Machine B
(7 configs), single-node halo_training with `TORCH_COMPILE_MODE=max-autotune-no-cudagraphs`.
Dataset: wikitext-103-odin32k.bin. Model: OdinFlat (121.7M params). 100 optimizer
steps per config (measured from step 30+).

**Headline findings:**

| Knob | Range | Effect | Action |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| `num_workers` | {4, 8, 12, 14} | **Flat** (±0.4%) | Set default = 12 (codification, no harm) |
| `batch × accum` | 12-config grid | batch dominates (+17% from 8→32), accum marginal (+3%) | Keep defaults; recommend batch=32 for memory-rich scenarios |
| `block_size` | {256, 512, 1024} | **block=512 wins +4%** over 256, +3% over 1024 | **Set default = 512** |

Single default change with clear evidence: `block_size: 256 → 512`.
Secondary codification: `num_workers: 4 → 12` (no throughput effect, but standardizes).

## Sweep 1 — num_workers

Fixed: `block=256, batch=16, accum=8, lr=8e-4`. Varying: `num_workers ∈ {4, 8, 12, 14}`.

| num_workers | tok/s | mem | wall/config |
|-:|-:|-:|-:|
| 4 | 19,630 | 6.1 GB | 209 s |
| 8 | 19,638 | 6.1 GB | 185 s |
| 12 | 19,585 | 6.1 GB | 184 s |
| 14 | 19,664 | 6.1 GB | 183 s |

**Range: 19,585 – 19,664 tok/s (±0.2%)**. Pre-tokenized memmap datasets have
negligible dataloader cost; worker count doesn't matter within 4–14. Wall-time
shrinks slightly from nw=4→8 (I/O pipelining) then plateaus.

## Sweep 2 — batch × accum (12-config cartesian)

Fixed: `block=256, num_workers=12, lr=8e-4`. Varying: `batch ∈ {8, 16, 32}`,
`accum ∈ {4, 8, 16, 32}`.

Throughput (tok/s):

|  | accum=4 | accum=8 | accum=16 | accum=32 |
|-:|-:|-:|-:|-:|
| **batch=8** | 17,457 | 18,124 | 18,334 | 18,491 |
| **batch=16** | 19,292 | 19,676 | 19,791 | 19,940 |
| **batch=32** | 20,142 | 20,372 | 20,353 | 20,468 |

Memory per microstep (invariant across accum):

| batch | mem |
|-:|-:|
| 8 | 4.3 GB |
| 16 | 6.1 GB |
| 32 | 9.8 GB |

### Interpretations

1. **batch dominates.** batch=32 is +5% over batch=16, which is +8% over batch=8
   at matching accum. The GPU starts saturating around batch=16; batch=32 gives
   smaller but real incremental gains.

2. **accum is marginal at single-node.** +3% from accum=4 to accum=32 at batch=16.
   In DDP, accum amortizes allreduce so the effect will be larger; single-node
   measurements understate the DDP win for larger accum.

3. **Best single-node config: batch=32, accum=32** (20,468 tok/s, 9.8 GB). But
   this is eff_batch=1024 sequences — too large for stable LLM training at typical
   LRs without scaling. Recommended practical config: **batch=16, accum=8 (current
   default)** or **batch=32, accum=8** if memory and LR scaling permit.

## Sweep 3 — block_size

Fixed: `batch=16, accum=8, num_workers=12, lr=8e-4`. Varying: `block ∈ {256, 512, 1024}`.

| block | tok/s | mem | wall/config |
|-:|-:|-:|-:|
| 256 | 19,599 | 6.1 GB | 182 s |
| **512** | **20,408** | 9.8 GB | 437 s |
| 1024 | 19,779 | 17.3 GB | 865 s |

**Surprise result: block=512 wins by +4% over block=256 at the same batch size.**
Per-token throughput is highest at block=512. block=1024 loses the advantage,
likely due to attention softmax memory pressure and reduced GPU occupancy at the
2× larger per-microstep tensor.

This validates the observation from the resumed-from-wikitext gpt-small training
run, which saw +2.5% throughput at block=512 vs the prior wikitext block=256 run.
The sweep measures it at +4% in a clean single-variable comparison.

**The earlier smoke-test finding (block=512 slower by ~5%)** was misleading — that
measurement included max-autotune compile warmup in the 200-step average. This
100-step sweep measures steps 30+, excluding the heaviest warmup.

## Changes committed

### `scripts/launch_ddp.sh` defaults
- `BLOCK=256 → BLOCK=512`
- `NUM_WORKERS=4 → NUM_WORKERS=12`

### `scripts/train_ddp.py` argparse defaults
- `--block-size` default 256 → 512
- `--num-workers` default 4 → 12

### Everything else unchanged
- batch_size=16, accum_steps=8 (current default is good middle-ground)
- warmup_steps=300, max_grad_norm=1.0 (not throughput variables)

## Context-dependent tunings (not defaults)

Documented in AGENTS.md. Users can override via env vars to `launch_ddp.sh`.

| Scenario | Recommended override |
|----------|---------------------|
| Memory-rich, max throughput | `BATCH=32 ACCUM=8` (+5% tok/s, ~2× memory) |
| Longer context needed | `BLOCK=1024` (−3% tok/s, ~2× memory) |
| Gradient quality paramount | `ACCUM=16` or `ACCUM=32` (larger eff_batch, smoother) |
| Low-memory environment | `BLOCK=256 BATCH=8` (4.3 GB footprint) |

## Methodology notes

- **Single-node single-process measurements.** Does not capture DDP allreduce effects.
  In DDP, larger accum should win more (halves allreduce frequency per optimizer step).
- **100 steps per config.** First 30 steps dropped as warmup; measurement from
  steps 30–100 (8 log_interval=10 samples).
- **Loss values ignored.** Short warmup produces unreliable losses (summed across
  accum steps, not normalized); throughput is the sweep target, not convergence.
- **Dataset small enough to fit in memmap.** Numbers should generalize to any
  pre-tokenized .bin dataset of similar vocab (32768).
- **Noise band ±0.4%** based on Sweep 1 (which varies only num_workers and showed
  effectively zero spread).
